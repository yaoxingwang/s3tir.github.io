Abstract

Thermal Infrared (TIR) imagery presents a unique challenge: the coexistence of diffusive smoothness driven by heat conduction and structural sharpness caused by material discontinuities. Existing RGB-based methods struggle to reconcile these properties.

We present S3-TIR, a framework that introduces a Unified Generalized-Exponential Gabor Primitive. This primitive explicitly couples an adaptive spatial support with internal spectral modulation. Furthermore, we propose a Frequency-Aware Deferred Splatting pipeline with a Multi-View Frequency Consistency Loss to resolve optimization ambiguities. Extensive experiments demonstrate that S3-TIR achieves state-of-the-art performance, significantly reducing jittering artifacts and signal leakage.

Method Overview

Our method consists of: (1) Unified Primitive Construction to decompose thermal signals; (2) Frequency-Aware Deferred Splatting to generate G-Buffers for sharpness and frequency; (3) Physics-Driven Optimization to enforce multi-view consistency.
