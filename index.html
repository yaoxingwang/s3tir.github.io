Abstract

We present S3-TIR (Coupled Spatial-Spectral Splatting), a physics-driven framework for high-fidelity thermal infrared (TIR) novel view synthesis. Existing 3D Gaussian splatting methods struggle to capture the unique duality of TIR imagery: the coexistence of diffusive smoothness from heat conduction and structural sharpness from material discontinuities.

To address this issue, we leverage the intrinsic frequency properties of thermal scenes. S3-TIR couples adaptive spatial support with internal spectral modulation via a unified generalized-exponential Gabor primitive. Furthermore, we propose a frequency-aware deferred splatting scheme that enforces multi-view frequency consistency. Experiments demonstrate that S3-TIR achieves state-of-the-art performance, notably reducing LPIPS by 42.5% on the RGBT-Scenes dataset.

Motivation: The Thermal Conflict

Standard Gaussians ($\beta=2$) act as low-pass filters, causing signal leakage at sharp thermal boundaries (Fig b). Our method employs a generalized-exponential support ($\beta=20$) to act as a physical truncation controller, strictly confining textures within geometry (Fig c).

Method Overview

Our pipeline: 1) Coupled Spatial-Spectral Representation: Initializing primitives with adaptive spatial support $\mathcal{S}$ and spectral field $\mathcal{F}$. 2) Frequency-Aware Deferred Splatting: Rasterizing intrinsic attributes into G-Buffers ($\Psi_{low}, \Psi_{high}, \Psi_{\beta}$). 3) Physics-Driven Optimization: Enforcing Multi-View Frequency Consistency.
