Abstract

We present S3-TIR (Coupled Spatial-Spectral Splatting), a physics-driven framework for high-fidelity thermal infrared (TIR) novel view synthesis. Existing 3D Gaussian splatting methods struggle to capture the unique duality of TIR imagery: the coexistence of diffusive smoothness from heat conduction and structural sharpness from material discontinuities.

To address this issue, we leverage the intrinsic frequency properties of thermal scenes. S3-TIR couples adaptive spatial support with internal spectral modulation via a unified generalized-exponential Gabor primitive. This design utilizes spatial support as a physical truncation controller, confining high-frequency textures within sharp boundaries while maintaining smooth diffusion elsewhere. Furthermore, we propose a frequency-aware deferred splatting scheme that enforces multi-view frequency consistency, anchoring frequency distributions to the 3D structure rather than view-dependent appearance. Experiments on diverse benchmarks demonstrate that S3-TIR achieves state-of-the-art performance, notably reducing LPIPS by 42.5% and improving PSNR by 3.2% on the RGBT-Scenes dataset.

Method Overview

Our pipeline consists of three key stages: 1) Coupled Spatial-Spectral Representation: We initialize Unified Primitives where an adaptive spatial support $\mathcal{S}$ (Generalized-Exponential) is coupled with a spectral modulation field $\mathcal{F}$ (Gabor). This allows physical truncation of textures at boundaries. 2) Frequency-Aware Deferred Splatting: Instead of rendering final color directly, we rasterize intrinsic attributes into intermediate G-Buffers: Low-Frequency Energy $\Psi_{low}$, High-Frequency Energy $\Psi_{high}$, and Spatial Sharpness $\Psi_{\beta}$. 3) Physics-Driven Optimization: We enforce Multi-View Frequency Consistency to anchor these frequency properties in 3D space, resolving the ambiguity between geometry and texture.
